# Trademarks

"Kaption" and the Kaption logo are trademarks of Michał Wojciechowski,
the project author. The licence in [`LICENSE`](../LICENSE) (AGPL-3.0 +
Commercial) covers source code rights. **It does not grant any rights to the Kaption name, the
Kaption logo, or related branding.** This page explains, in plain
language, what is and isn't OK.

## OK without asking

- Truthfully describing your project as "based on Kaption", "uses
  Kaption", "compatible with Kaption", or "a fork of Kaption" — as long
  as the description is honest and your project itself is not branded
  as Kaption.
- Linking to <https://kaption.one> in documentation, blog posts,
  articles, and reviews.
- Using a screenshot of Kaption in a review, tutorial, or article.
- Distributing the official, unmodified Kaption binaries and installers
  exactly as we publish them.

## Not OK without a written trademark licence

- Calling your fork "Kaption-PL", "Kaption-HSR", "FreeKaption",
  "Kaption Pro", "Kaption Plus", or any other name that includes
  "Kaption" or a near-miss spelling.
- Using the Kaption icon, wordmark, or any close visual variant on your
  fork's installer, app icon, GitHub repository banner, marketing site,
  Discord server, or store listing.
- Selling or distributing modified versions of Kaption under the
  Kaption name.
- Implying that your fork is endorsed by, sponsored by, or affiliated
  with the Kaption project when it isn't.

## If you fork Kaption

If you fork Kaption and ship the fork to other users — even for free —
you must:

1. Give your fork a clearly different name. Not "Kaption", not
   "Kaption-something", not a near-misspelling.
2. Replace the Kaption icon and any Kaption branding inside the app —
   window titles, About box, log files, installer name, file
   associations, system-tray tooltip.
3. Update the README and any user-visible text so it is clear your fork
   is not the official Kaption project.

The AGPL-3.0 obligations on your fork (publish source under AGPL-3.0)
remain in force regardless of branding.

## Why we care

Kaption is a small indie project. Most users find us via Discord and
word-of-mouth. A rebranded fork that keeps the Kaption name confuses
users about what is officially supported, what is safe to install, and
who to contact for help. Forks are welcome — just give them their own
identity.
